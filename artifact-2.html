Artifact 2: Incorporating Evidence Practice

In-Class Group Activity  ·  February 11, 2026  ·  ENC2135

What this artifact is: A group in-class activity completed on February 11, 2026. Students were given four poorly integrated quotation examples, each named after a common citation mistake ("I Swear I Did Some Research," "Armadillo Roadkill," "Am I in the Right Movie," "Dating Spiderman"). Groups were asked to identify the specific problem in each example and rewrite it correctly. The response below is from Nate Bock, whose group Drew Arfa was a member of.

The Prompt

Each of these attempts to incorporate quotes are annoying. Identify in which way(s) they are annoying, then correct them.

Group Submission (Nate Bock's Group)

Error Type Original Problem Corrected Version
I Swear I Did Some Research
Missing attribution on paraphrase 		The Seattle Seahawks crushed the New England Patriots in Super Bowl LX. They finished the night with a score of 29-13, after the Seattle Seahawks demonstrated their defensive strengths. [URL only, no in-text citation] Fixed
Barrows explains that the Seattle Seahawks defeated the New England Patriots in Super Bowl LX with a final score of 29–13. Their defense played a major role in the win and kept the Patriots from gaining any real momentum throughout the game.
Armadillo Roadkill
Quote dropped without introduction 		Just days after winning three Grammys, Bad Bunny performed at Super Bowl LX in a dynamic and star-studded halftime show. "Then he crashed through the roof — Daddy Yankee's 'Gasolina' played for a moment, clearly an acknowledgement and celebration of the Puerto Rican artists who laid the path for his career to go global." (Sherman). Fixed
Just days after winning three Grammys, Bad Bunny performed at Super Bowl LX during the halftime show. At one point he crashed through the roof while Daddy Yankee's "Gasolina" played. Sherman explains that this moment was a tribute to the Puerto Rican artists who helped him reach global success (Sherman).
Am I in the Right Movie
Quote doesn't fit grammatically into the sentence 		Sherman describes one such moment in her article, "a surprise Lady Gaga emerged at a real wedding scene, singing her portion of the Bruno Mars-duet 'Die with a Smile,' joined by the salsa band Los Sobrinos." (Sherman). Fixed
The halftime show included a lot of celebrity appearances and also had some surprises. Sherman describes one moment when Lady Gaga unexpectedly appeared during a real wedding scene and sang her part of the Bruno Mars duet "Die with a Smile" alongside the salsa band Los Sobrinos (Sherman).
Dating Spiderman
Paragraph opens and closes with a quote, no analysis 		"If you don't eat Pringles as fast as humanly possible, it was either because you were putting two of them together in your mouth to make a duck face or because you got your hand stuck in the tube" (Rosenberg). However, Sabrina Carpenter shows a new way to interact with Pringles in a notable 2026 Super Bowl LX commercial. Fixed
In a humorous 2026 Super Bowl LX commercial, Rosenberg jokes that if you were not eating Pringles as fast as possible, it was probably because you were stacking two chips to make a duck face or got your hand stuck in the can (Rosenberg). In another 2026 Super Bowl ad, Sabrina Carpenter presents a different and more creative way to enjoy Pringles (Rosenberg).

Why This Artifact

This activity showed me something that is genuinely easy to miss in an early draft: a source does not argue for you just because you put it in the paragraph. A quote sitting in the middle of a paragraph with no setup is not evidence, it is just text. Our group had to work through four specific examples of this going wrong, and actually naming what was broken in each one before we could fix it made the lesson land in a way that just reading about citation errors would not have.

That came back to me directly when I was revising the open letter. The first draft had both the Edelman Trust Barometer and behavioral economics research in it, but neither source was actually doing anything useful. They were cited, but they were not framed. A reader could get to those citations and still have no idea why the data mattered to the specific ask the letter was making. The revision fixed that by building context around each source before it appeared and then explaining what to take from it. That is exactly the pattern this activity taught, and I would not have seen the problem in my own draft clearly enough to fix it without having gone through the exercise first.
