What this artifact is: A discussion board contribution from the In-Class Rhetorical Rationale Practice, completed with classmates during March 2026. The activity required students to articulate and defend the specific rhetorical decisions made in their own writing, explaining why each choice was made for a particular audience, purpose, and context.

This artifact reflects the transition from writing by instinct to writing by analysis. Why This Artifact

The Rhetorical Rationale Practice was the activity that made visible how much of my writing had been produced through instinct rather than deliberate analysis. Explaining why I had chosen a particular tone for a particular audience, or why I had positioned evidence in a certain order, required me to reconstruct reasoning I had not consciously applied when writing. In many cases, I could not reconstruct it, which told me something important: those choices had been made without the analysis they needed.

That recognition changed how I drafted from that point forward. The open letter revision was the clearest example. When I returned to the first draft, I was not asking whether it sounded right. I was asking whether each sentence was doing the specific work that the genre and audience required. That is the question the Rhetorical Rationale Practice taught me to ask, and it is the question that made the revision substantive rather than cosmetic.
